01 / About

Research grounded in signals, systems, and real constraints.

My research sits at the intersection of representation learning and intelligent sensing. I am interested in models that can discover reusable structure from temporal data instead of being rebuilt for every dataset and task.

Before beginning my PhD, I worked on heterogeneous large-model training, multimodal perception, LiDAR deployment, and industrial signal analysis. These experiences shape how I approach foundation models: performance matters, but so do robustness, efficiency, and clear evaluation.

02 / Research

Current research agenda

I study how a single pretrained model can understand diverse temporal processes and transfer reliably to new tasks.

01

Foundation models

Universal temporal representations

Pretraining objectives and architectures that capture transferable structure across domains, variables, sampling rates, and forecasting horizons.

02

Representation learning

Multi-scale sequence modeling

Adaptive patching, frequency-aware features, and multi-resolution mechanisms for sequences with interacting local and long-range dynamics.

03

Efficient AI

Adaptation without full retraining

Parameter-efficient adaptation and compact inference strategies that make large temporal models useful beyond high-resource compute environments.

04

Reliability

Generalization under imperfect data

Evaluation under distribution shift, missing observations, irregular sampling, and limited labels—with an emphasis on honest transfer claims.
